### Best Practices

1. **Preparation Timing**
   - Start preparing 10-15 minutes before scheduled time
   - Ensure food is fresh when customer arrives

2. **Car Identification**
   - Use car model and number to locate customer
   - Confirm customer name before handing over order

3. **Service Quality (Eat in Car)**
   - Provide water promptly
   - Check if customer needs anything
   - Collect tray after reasonable time

4. **Communication**
   - Call customer if running late
   - Inform about any item unavailability

## Tips & Tricks

### For Customers
- **Book in Advance**: Schedule during off-peak hours for faster service
- **Choose Wisely**: Select "Eat in Car" for a relaxed meal, "Takeaway" for quick pickup
- **Be Punctual**: Arrive within 10 minutes of scheduled time
- **Park Properly**: Park in designated car dine-in area if available

### For Staff
- **Prepare Early**: Have orders ready 5 minutes before scheduled time
- **Stay Organized**: Keep car dine-in orders separate for easy identification
- **Be Proactive**: Offer service before customer asks (for Eat in Car mode)
- **Maintain Quality**: Ensure food temperature and presentation

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I change my service mode after placing the order?
A: Contact the restaurant directly to request changes.

### Q: What if I'm running late?
A: Call the restaurant to inform them. They'll keep your order warm.

### Q: Is there a parking fee?
A: Check with the restaurant for parking policies.

### Q: Can I order for multiple cars?
A: Place separate orders for each car with different car details.

### Q: What if my car details are wrong?
A: Contact the restaurant immediately with correct details.

### Q: How long can I stay in the car after eating?
A: Be considerate of other customers. Typically 30-45 minutes is reasonable.
